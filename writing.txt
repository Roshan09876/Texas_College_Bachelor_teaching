The diagram illustrates the process of generating electricity through hydropower 
in the Hydroelectric power station using numerous steps.

Overall, it is notable that, the steps required for the production of electricity are different for
 both day and night time. However, machinery items are same to produce it. While initial step begins
  with high level reservoir river during day time, it is opposite during night, where water is passed
   from low level reservoir river.

To begin, dam is constructed in the river to control the flow of water with Intake in the bottom part.
 During day time, intake remained open and water is passed through penstock by controlling the flow to
  the reversible turbine which works as operator generator. In contrast, During night time, dam intake
   is closed and supply of water starts from Low-level reservoir river to the reversible turbines which
    pumps water into high level reservoir to generate electricity. Moreover, after getting water supply
     process of producing electricity is same for both timezone. Furthermore, generator starts its work 
     inside powerhouse and pass electricity to the national grid with the help of power lines.
